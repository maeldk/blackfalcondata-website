Clean, normalized, AI-ready.

Every actor writes to the same Apify dataset format. Pick your output: JSON for code, CSV/Excel for analysts, JSON Lines for streaming, or call the actor as an MCP tool from your AI agent.

Field names are consistent across every job board, every country. No re-mapping. No schema drift between sources. ISO 8601 dates. ISO 3166 country codes. Currency-coded salaries.

  • id stable across re-runs
  • posted_at ISO 8601 UTC
  • title · company · location always populated
  • salary_min · salary_max · currency parsed where present
  • description_text · description_html full body
  • apply_url · contact_email · contact_phone extracted where available
  • tags · skills · is_remote · seniority normalized
  • change_type new · changed · removed (incremental mode)

Aggregator · 46 sources

2.8M jobs from 46 ATS platforms, in one schema

How fast can you build a custom scraper?
Most custom scrapers ship in 5–7 days from a confirmed scope. Discovery and feasibility review come back within 24 hours of you sending us the source URL. We don’t bill for the discovery itself.
What anti-bot defenses can you handle?
Cloudflare Bot Management, DataDome, PerimeterX, Akamai Bot Manager, F5 Shape, image/reCAPTCHA/hCaptcha/Arkose challenges. We use residential proxy rotation, session bootstrapping, and stealth-instrumented Playwright/Camoufox when needed. If a target is genuinely uncrackable within reasonable cost we say so in discovery — we don’t bill for impossible work.
Where does the scraper run?
Default deployment is Apify Store — under your own account or ours, your choice. We can also ship the source as a self-hostable Docker image for your own infrastructure (AWS Lambda, Fly.io, Kubernetes, bare metal). MCP packaging is included by default so your AI agents can call the scraper directly as a tool.
Do I own the code?
Yes. Custom-build engagements transfer full ownership of the scraper source code to you. We retain no rights to redistribute or resell it. We will keep maintenance access only if you’ve engaged us for ongoing SLA.
What if the source site changes its anti-bot or schema?
Every actor we maintain has a canary monitor that runs daily and alerts us when schema drifts or anti-bot defenses change. Custom builds include 30 days of free post-launch maintenance. Ongoing SLA contracts cover incident response within agreed windows (typically 4–24h depending on tier).
How is data delivered?
REST API endpoint, dataset export (JSON, CSV, Excel, XML), streaming JSON Lines for backfills, or as an MCP tool that AI agents can call directly. You pick whichever fits your pipeline — we don’t lock you into one format.
What does it cost?
Custom-build pricing depends on source complexity (anti-bot defenses, JS rendering, auth, schema depth, expected volume). Existing actors on Apify Store have transparent per-event pricing on each actor page. Send us a feasibility request and we quote a fixed price within 24 hours — no commitment.
Is scraping legal?
Scraping publicly accessible data is generally legal in most jurisdictions and is repeatedly upheld in U.S. and EU case law (hiQ v. LinkedIn, Ryanair v. PR Aviation, etc.). We respect robots.txt, rate-limit to avoid impacting target sites, and don’t bypass login walls without explicit client authorization. GDPR considerations are part of every discovery conversation when personal data is involved.
Can you handle very large volumes?
Yes. Apify scales horizontally; we have actors handling millions of records per run. For very large jobs we typically architect with partition keys, incremental delta tracking, and resumable state — so a 50M-listing crawl can stop and restart without re-scraping. Send us your expected volume and we’ll architect for it.
Can you take over a scraper someone else built?
Yes — we frequently take over abandoned or breaking scrapers from internal teams and prior contractors. We audit the existing code, document field-level mappings, harden the anti-bot layer, and migrate to a maintainable structure. Pricing is hourly for the audit, fixed for the rebuild.
